# Dieter Rams Scorecard

1. **Good design is innovative — 2/3**
   - Evidence: potion sorting drives damage, mana, objectives, intents, route choice, and roguelike rewards (`01-evidence.md`, Structural).
   - Justification: it refreshes familiar water-sort and roguelike patterns with a coherent combat connection, but does not establish a wholly new interaction category.

2. **Good design makes a product useful — 2/3**
   - Evidence: New Run, Continue, route choice, and the whole battle loop are directly accessible, but navigation duplicates actions and the core board is pointer-only (`01-evidence.md`, Structural/Visual).
   - Justification: the primary task completes, although duplicated routes and missing non-pointer control add avoidable friction.

3. **Good design is aesthetic — 1/3**
   - Evidence: a coherent fantasy language coexists with 127 literal colors, 22 font sizes, 9px copy, and low-contrast map states (`01-evidence.md`, Visual).
   - Justification: the art direction is strong, but more than five system-level inconsistencies prevent a higher score.

4. **Good design makes a product understandable — 1/3**
   - Evidence: HERO opens Credits, Continue/Map can silently start runs, currency types are merged, and bonus goals omit required information (`01-evidence.md`, Copy).
   - Justification: multiple primary labels require interpretation or contradict behavior.

5. **Good design is unobtrusive — 1/3**
   - Evidence: ornate frames repeat across nearly every band while the battle stacks many informational layers (`01-evidence.md`, Structural/Visual).
   - Justification: decoration frequently competes with gameplay content instead of receding.

6. **Good design is honest — 1/3**
   - Evidence: hidden event costs, false save durability copy, an inaccurate Abandon label, and skill/ultimate claim mismatches (`01-evidence.md`, Copy).
   - Justification: there is no monetization dark pattern, but several material label-to-behavior mismatches remain.

7. **Good design is long-lasting — 2/3**
   - Evidence: the dark-fantasy alchemy language is internally recognizable, but all areas reuse one crypt environment and key trees are hand-built in large scripts (`01-evidence.md`, Structural/Visual).
   - Justification: the brand can age well, while the current one-environment implementation limits extension.

8. **Good design is thorough — 1/3**
   - Evidence: success/disabled states exist, but loading/error/focus states are incomplete and armor-break objectives cannot progress (`01-evidence.md`, Visual/Copy).
   - Justification: at least three important states or edge paths are missing or broken.

9. **Good design is environmentally friendly — 1/3**
   - Evidence: reduced-effects support exists, yet idle redraw timers and runtime PCM stem generation continue (`01-evidence.md`, Visual/Resource).
   - Justification: attention to effects exists, but CPU, memory, and package budgets are not enforced.

10. **Good design is as little design as possible — 1/3**
    - Evidence: duplicated menu destinations, dense battle bands, and repeated ornate containers (`01-evidence.md`, Structural/Visual).
    - Justification: more than five removable or consolidatable elements remain across the primary surfaces.

**Total: 13/30**
