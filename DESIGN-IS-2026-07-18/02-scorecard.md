# Dieter Rams Scorecard

1. Good design is innovative — Score: 2/3
   Evidence: Water-sort is meaningfully combined with combat, builds, signatures, bosses, and route decisions, but the seven-depth macro loop remains fixed.
   Justification: It refreshes a familiar puzzle with a clear gameplay improvement without yet establishing a wholly new interaction pattern.

2. Good design makes a product useful — Score: 2/3
   Evidence: The run flow is direct and resumable, but duplicated Hall actions and dense secondary HUD add friction.
   Justification: The primary task works well, while adjacent surfaces add avoidable decisions and reading cost.

3. Good design is aesthetic — Score: 1/3
   Evidence: Premium art is strong, but 172 literal colors, non-token spacing/type, tiny map copy, flat halos, and boss-label overlap create more than five inconsistencies.
   Justification: One strong art direction is present, but execution is not yet governed by one visible UI system.

4. Good design makes a product understandable — Score: 1/3
   Evidence: Hero→Credits, count-based “Synergy,” hardcoded Offline, tooltip-only details, and 9–12px tactical/map copy obscure multiple controls or concepts.
   Justification: Tutorial support helps, but several important labels still require explanation or do not match behavior.

5. Good design is unobtrusive — Score: 1/3
   Evidence: Ten-plus battle information bands and ornate chrome compete with the puzzle and force secondary text to shrink.
   Justification: Decoration frequently competes with content instead of consistently receding behind it.

6. Good design is honest — Score: 1/3
   Evidence: Daily reward/global claims, Hero navigation, Offline state, Synergy naming, and persisted Reduced Effects each diverge from actual behavior.
   Justification: Multiple inflations and label/behavior mismatches exceed the rubric’s allowance for a higher score.

7. Good design is long-lasting — Score: 2/3
   Evidence: Alchemy fantasy suits the genre, but excessive skeuomorphic variants create one notable dated/maintenance marker.
   Justification: The brand should age well if its frame language is consolidated and restrained.

8. Good design is thorough down to the last detail — Score: 1/3
   Evidence: Loading/retry is missing, focus and touch targets are inconsistent, visual tests miss overlaps, and initial boards are not solver-verified.
   Justification: Several edge states and verification layers remain incomplete despite good save and gameplay test coverage.

9. Good design is environmentally friendly — Score: 1/3
   Evidence: Motion can be reduced, but APK/assets are heavy, unused art may ship, five systems animate continuously, and runtime performance is unmeasured.
   Justification: Resource restraint exists in isolated systems but is not enforced across the Android production pipeline.

10. Good design is as little design as possible — Score: 1/3
    Evidence: Duplicate navigation, repeated hidden cards, dense tactical copy, and decorative variants can be removed without harming the task.
    Justification: More than five elements or treatments can be simplified while preserving capability.

**Total: 13/30**
